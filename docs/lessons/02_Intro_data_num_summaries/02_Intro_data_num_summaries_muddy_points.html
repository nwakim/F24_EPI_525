3 What benefit would the median provide, especially if the mean would be more representative of the entire dataset including any outliers? Is this relating directly to how you mentioned that some people choose to not include outliers (also I thought that was being selective to data to leave out data points)?

Okay, good question! First, the mean would not really be “more representative” of the data. It just represents something different than the median. Both are aiming to summarize the center of the data. So if we have one observation that is an outlier, but the rest of the group is pretty condensed, then which measure best represents the center of the data? The mean does not do a very good job of identifying where most observations are, but the median captures the center much better.

Honestly, I would not dwell on removing outliers. Many statisticians would agree that we do not remove outliers to make our mean a better representation. And in general, it’s not a good idea to use math as the evidence to make the math better. Ya know?
